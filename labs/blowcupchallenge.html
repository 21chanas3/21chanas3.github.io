Blow Cup Challenge

Introduction

In this experiment, we will be attempting to find out what are the optimal conditions to perform the Blow Cup Challenge in. The Blow Cup Challenge involves three cups, a launch cup, projectile cup and a target cup. The aim is to get the projectile cup from the launch cup to the target cup, by blowing in space between the launch and projectile cup.

Hypothesis

I predict that if we use a large solo cup filled with 75% water as the launch cup and place the target cup 5 cm away from the launch cup, it will be optimal for the challenge. This is because I think by filling it up to the 75% mark, it requires less distance to launch out of the cup. I think placing it 5cm away would be optimal because we might blow the cup a bit too hard.

Procedures

Observation

  • Tests with water at 50% were conducted first, then tests with water at 75%.
  • Tests with distance at 1 cm were conducted first, then test with distance at 5 cm.
  • Each time a cup is successfully blown out of the launch cup, a small amount water from the launch cup is also ejected
